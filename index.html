ricardo jorge lopes lab

The Ricardo Jorge Lopes Lab at the FCUL uses birds to study the importance of key traits on the evolution and long-term conservation of biodiversity. These range from coloration and trophic interactions to host-parasite systems. As Curator of the Avian Collection of the National Museum of Natural History and Science, we promote the value of avian specimens for research.

We mainly work with isolated, insular, fragmented, migratory and domesticated species, especially in Iberia, North Africa and Macaronesia.
